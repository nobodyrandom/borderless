ABOUT US

OUR MISSION

BORDERLESS is a grassroots, non-partisan, non-profit organization which uses creative means of expression to draw attention to, educate on, and raise funds for often overlooked internally displaced persons (IDP) and refugee crises around the world by bringing together people of various backgrounds.

Our long-term vision is to be able to provide medical and general assistance to areas in immediate need. Funds raised by BORDERLESS and other student clubs, societies, and organizations would be sent to regions with large numbers of refugees that do not receive sufficient aid.

ABOUT US

BORDERLESS evolved from the fact that Ukrainian refugees and IDPs were being overshadowed by the geopolitics of the recent war. Initially, the purpose of this project was to fundraise for a cause important to every member of our team, with a direct connection to the country for one. However, by bringing the Ukrainian refugee crisis to the forefront, there was the realization that there are other crises that are not receiving enough attention.

As a result, we are committed to spotlighting a different refugee crisis every year, to bring it the attention it deserves.

Today, our team consists of University of Toronto undergraduate students who all agree that the severity of the issue is disproportionate to the attention it is receiving. We share a common goal of putting the people first. This year, we are partnering with UNHCR in order to better facilitate the aid of those directly affected by the crisis.

OUR ORIGINS

BORDERLESS originated as a fundraising and social advocacy program with the assistance of the non-profit organization Broadening Horizons. Through their 2014/2015 Making It Happen program they provided both leadership development and a micro-grant to help us launch our initiative.

OUR SUPPORTERS

Broadening Horizons is a youth empowerment agency that seeks to educate and empower youth to create change within themselves and globally. Their goal is to bring awareness to global and local issues from a youth-based perspective while empowering youth to become Agents of Change.

Our faculty advisor is Dr. Arne Kislenko. Dr. Kislenko is Adjunct Professor in the International Relations Program at Trinity College/the Munk School for International Studies at the University of Toronto and Programme Director and Associate Professor of History at Ryerson University.

OUR LOGO

One of our primary challenges was finding a simple, yet effective way of expressing our mission through our logo. We wanted a logo that didn’t require too much thinking to understand, yet wasn’t as cliché as a globe with no political borders drawn. However, we wanted to stick to the idea of a globe and same time, convey the message of "borderless". The mission is to assist refugees, of whatever country of origin and wherever they may be, in an attempt to focus on alleviating their plight without any political consideration.

This logo is our attempt at abolishing borders in every sense. Continents and the oceans between then can be considered borders, as they separate nations and peoples using natural geographical lines. This logo shows no continents, with no such boundaries between the people. This logo portrays a world in which there are no geographical divides between north and south, east and west. The strip of land encompasses the circumference of the globe, connecting everyone in the true sense of being a world without borders.
